1 Descartes’s argument

In this section I check the proofs given in text

\begin{equation} \forall y \lnot(y > d),\lnot(c = d), \forall y(Rcy \to \exists x Sxy), Rcd, \forall x \forall y(Sxy \to x \geqslant y) \vdash \lnot Scd \land \exists x(Sxd \land (x = d)) \end{equation}

That is formula (9) .

To check the previous proof, copy the latex code below (where predicates B and E stand for respectively relations \( > \) and \( = \)) 

(\forall y \lnot(Byd) \land \lnot Ecd  \land \forall y(Rcy \rightarrow \exists x
Sxy ) \land Rcd \land \forall  x \forall y(Sxy \rightarrow (Bxy \lor Exy)))\rightarrow
(\lnot Scd \land \exists x(Sxd \land Exd))

and, to test the validity this formula that is a logical consequence of \eqref{eleven}, paste it into the Tree Proof Generator. 

imogen fol prove -i "(forall Y.(~b(Y,d)) &  ~e(c,d) & forall Y.(r(c,Y) -> exists X.(s(X,Y))) & r(c,d) & forall X.forall Y.(s(X,Y) -> (b(X,Y) | e(X,Y)))) -> (~s(c,d) & exists X.(s(X,d) & e(X,d)))"
